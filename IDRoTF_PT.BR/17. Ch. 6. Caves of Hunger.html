Chapter 6: Caves of Hunger

Após abrir uma fissura na Geleira Reghed, um grupo de exploradores se prepara para entrar.

Equipados com a "Rima da Donzela de Gelo", os personagens podem começar sua busca pela cidade perdida de Netherese enterrada nas profundezas da Geleira Reghed. Idealmente, os personagens devem estar pelo menos no 8º nível quando embarcarem nesta jornada. Personagens de nível inferior podem sobreviver às Cavernas da Fome se forem cautelosos e descansarem com frequência para recuperar seus pontos de vida e magia gastos.

Os personagens devem usar a "Rima da Donzela de Gelo" para abrir uma fenda na parede glacial em um local onde uma cachoeira antes descia pela geleira. A passagem recém-formada leva às Cavernas da Fome, uma masmorra que foi selada pela Donzela de Gelo há muito tempo — uma rede de cavernas de gelo sepulcrais assombradas por seres vorazes. Essas câmaras devem ser navegadas com sucesso para alcançar a cidade Netherese de Ythryn.

Após abrir uma fissura na Geleira Reghed, um grupo de exploradores se prepara para entrar.

Running This Chapter

Este capítulo se concentra na exploração das cavernas de gelo pelos personagens. Há uma boa chance de que @creature[Vellynne Harpell|IDRotF] também esteja presente, servindo como guia NPC. Se ela morreu no início da aventura, os personagens podem usar @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] como um guia substituto, supondo que eles recuperaram o orbe do professor de Vellynne da ilha de Auril em @UUID[.0000000000000015#i3.-nass-lantomirs-ghost]{capítulo 5}. Se nem Vellynne nem Professor Skant estiverem por perto, você pode fornecer a outro NPC as informações necessárias para chegar às Cavernas da Fome. Os candidatos prováveis incluem @creature[Hengar Aesnvaard|IDRotF] (veja "Escolta da Tribo Elk" abaixo) e @creature[Dzaan's simulacrum|IDRotF] (veja @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{apêndice C}).

Character Advancement

Os personagens devem ganhar um nível quando chegarem a @UUID[.0000000000000016#h39.-passage-to-ythryn]{área H39} pela primeira vez, independentemente da rota pela qual chegarem lá. Assim, se eles estavam no 8º nível no início deste capítulo, estarão no 9º nível quando entrarem em Ythryn pela primeira vez.

Race to the Glacier

Se @creature[Vellynne Harpell|IDRotF] sobreviveu aos perigos da ilha de Auril e retornou a Icewind Dale com os personagens, ela os incentiva a acompanhá-la até a geleira sem demora, para que um de seus rivais na Arcane Brotherhood não os alcance. Elk and Tiger

Com grandes eventos tendo sido postos em movimento, aqueles sensíveis à profecia começaram a experimentar sonhos e visões. Uma dessas pessoas é @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF], um xamã de cinquenta anos da Tribo Reghed Elk, que recebeu uma visão sobre a jornada dos personagens até a Geleira Reghed.

Elk Tribe Escort

Várias horas antes do grupo chegar à geleira, leia:

Ao subir outra cordilheira, você avista uma dúzia de humanoides se aproximando, seus mantos esvoaçando ao vento que sopra da geleira oriental.

As figuras que se aproximam são dez @creature[Tribal Warrior||guerreiros tribais] da Tribo Elk, liderados por @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] (humano neutro @creature[druid] com &Reference[skill=Survival] +4) e @creature[Hengar Aesnvaard|IDRotF] (humano neutro bom @creature[gladiator] com &Reference[skill=Survival] +5). @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] acena para os personagens para sinalizar suas intenções pacíficas. Se eles estiverem dispostos a conversar, ele os direciona para uma alcova rochosa onde ambos os grupos podem desfrutar de um descanso do vento.

@creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] prossegue explicando sua visão e alerta sobre um grave perigo à frente:

"Tigres com gelo em suas veias vêm para derramar seu sangue sobre a neve. Eles farão tudo ao seu alcance para impedir que você encontre o que a Donzela de Gelo preservou sob o gelo."

@creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] e seus companheiros da tribo Elk se oferecem para escoltar o grupo até o pé da Geleira Reghed. Se os personagens recusarem essa oferta, os membros da tribo Elk retornam de onde vieram. @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] lhes deseja boa sorte e diz: "O destino do vale está sobre seus ombros."

Tiger Tribe Ambush

Quando os personagens chegarem à parede glacial, leia:

A Geleira Reghed se eleva a centenas de metros no céu. O pé da geleira ainda está a oitocentos metros de distância.

De repente, quase duas dúzias de figuras irrompem da neve a sessenta pés de distância de você. Eles estão espaçados a aproximadamente três metros de distância e começam a avançar em linha.

Personagens que têm uma pontuação passiva de Sabedoria (&Reference[skill=Perception]) de 15 ou mais não são @status[surprised] pela emboscada, nem são membros da Tribo Elk que estão viajando com o grupo.

Ambushers. @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] não foi o único que previu o caminho dos personagens. A rainha da Tribo do Tigre, @creature[Bjornhild Solvigsdottir|IDRotF] (veja @UUID[.0000000000000021#queen-bjornhild-solvigsdottir]{apêndice C}), também foi impelida à ação. Bjornhild adora Auril e está furiosa porque o grupo decidiu se intrometer nos assuntos da Donzela de Gelo. Se Bjornhild morreu no início da aventura, substitua-a por um @creature[frost druid|IDRotF] (veja @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{apêndice C}) chamado Ajka.

Se Bjornhild lidera a emboscada, ela é acompanhada por seu animal de estimação @creature[saber-toothed tiger], Grava. Se Ajka lidera a emboscada, dê a ela um companheiro @creature[rhinoceros] lanoso. Vinte @creature[Tribal Warrior||guerreiros tribais] compõem o resto da força de emboscada. Antes de se aproximarem para o combate corpo a corpo, eles lançam lanças nos personagens.

Elk Tribe Reaction. Se os personagens permitiram que @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF], Hengar e os outros membros da tribo Elk os acompanhassem, os dois líderes estão confiantes em sua capacidade de lutar contra os atacantes enquanto os personagens correm em direção à geleira. Se os personagens deixarem os membros da Tribo Elk defenderem os Tigres, o resultado da batalha depende se Bjornhild lidera a emboscada:

  • Se Bjornhild lidera a emboscada, tanto ela quanto Grava sobrevivem, assim como Hengar. Todos os três são reduzidos à metade de seus pontos de vida (arredondados para baixo). Todos os outros são mortos. Bjornhild faz Hengar prisioneiro e interpreta a fuga dos personagens como um sinal de que Auril pretende matá-los ela mesma; consequentemente, Bjornhild não os persegue.
  • Se Ajka lidera a emboscada, ela e Hengar são os únicos sobreviventes com metade de seus pontos de vida restantes. Após um breve impasse, Ajka se transforma em uma coruja da neve e foge, deixando o ferido Hengar sozinho.

Personagens que se recusam a deixar a Tribo Elk lutar esta batalha sem eles são atacados por Bjornhild e seu tigre dentes de sabre (ou Ajka e seu rinoceronte) enquanto Hengar e seus companheiros guerreiros da Tribo Elk se enfrentam com o resto da força de ataque. Você pode deixar de fazer jogadas de ataque para os guerreiros tribais; suponha que todos eles ajam na mesma contagem de iniciativa e que um guerreiro de cada tribo morra a cada rodada no turno daquela tribo. Hengar mata um guerreiro adicional da Tribo do Tigre em cada um de seus turnos. @creature[Mjenir|IDRotF] fica fora do combate corpo a corpo e usa seu truque @spell[produce flame] para lançar fogo nos inimigos.

Opening the Way

Quando os personagens chegarem à parede da Geleira Reghed no local especificado por @creature[Vellynne Harpell|IDRotF] ou Professor Skant, leia:

Uma imensa e brilhante parede de gelo azul se ergue diante de você. Parece impenetrável, mas então você percebe que uma faixa de cor mais clara quase divide a parede ao meio em um ponto. Esta deve ser a cachoeira congelada de que Vellynne falou!

Vellynne ou @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] incita um dos personagens a ler em voz alta a "Rima da Donzela de Gelo" (veja @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000023]{apêndice E}). Ler o poema em voz alta enquanto está diante da geleira tem o seguinte efeito:

Quando as últimas palavras do poema são ditas, um som estrondoso vem de dentro da geleira. O ruído atinge um crescendo quando a grande parede de gelo se divide. Através da rachadura, você pode ver uma abertura que leva para dentro da geleira.

Uma vez que o caminho está aberto, o grupo está livre para descer para o túnel recém-formado (@UUID[.0000000000000016#h1.-into-the-glacier]{área H1}).

Into the Caves of Hunger

Quando a cidade flutuante de Ythryn despencou do céu para a Geleira Reghed séculos atrás, o impacto da colisão (e o calor resultante) conduziu a cidade para mais fundo em seu coração até que, finalmente, ela parou. Auril então enterrou a cidade sob o gelo para preservá-la, reivindicando-a como sua.

As Cavernas da Fome são um complexo de masmorras বিস্তৃত esculpido na Geleira Reghed. Algumas de suas cavernas e túneis se formaram naturalmente, algumas se formaram como um subproduto da queda de Ythryn, e outras ainda são obra de criaturas como remorhazes que podem cavar ou derreter seu caminho através do gelo sólido. O fato de que todo o complexo é feito de gelo contribui para sua atmosfera alienígena. O ar é frequentemente viciado, o som se comporta de forma estranha, e a tensão de se mover cada vez mais para baixo desgasta a sanidade até mesmo dos aventureiros mais ousados.

General Features

As seguintes características são comuns em todas as Cavernas da Fome:

  • Ceilings. A maioria dos tetos das cavernas tem 9 metros de altura e são feitos de gelo liso. Os tetos dos túneis variam de 3 a 4,5 metros de altura, a menos que indicado de outra forma.

  • Darkness. Todas as áreas estão em completa escuridão, a menos que indicado de outra forma.

Echoes. O som viaja rapidamente aqui. Todas as verificações de Sabedoria (&Reference[skill=Perception]) para ouvir sons nessas cavernas têm vantagem.

  • Ice Slides. Conforme os remorhazes perfuram o gelo, o calor que irradia de seus corpos alisa as laterais dos túneis atrás deles até um acabamento semelhante a vidro. Muitas dessas passagens são severamente inclinadas, criando deslizamentos de gelo. Descer um deslizamento de gelo é fácil; escalar um é outra questão. Um personagem precisa de crampons ou outro equipamento de escalada para subir uma dessas passagens escorregadias.

Se os personagens têm @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] com eles, o orbe do professor pode fornecer comentários educativos sobre certas características encontradas nas Cavernas da Fome, conforme observado no texto, desde que o orbe esteja em uma posição onde possa ver seus arredores.

Psychic Hauntings

As Cavernas da Fome têm uma maneira de capturar e reter traumas psíquicos. Em certos locais, o texto pedirá que você verifique se há uma assombração psíquica, nesse caso, role um [[/r d20]] e consulte a tabela Assombrações Psíquicas para determinar se a área contém uma assombração. Se houver uma, sua imagem ou seu efeito não podem deixar a caverna em que são encontrados.

Tekeli-li

De todas as criaturas que reivindicam as Cavernas da Fome como seu covil, talvez a mais temível seja Tekeli-li, um @creature[gnoll vampire|IDRotF] (veja @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{apêndice C}).

Quando eles abriram a Geleira Reghed, os personagens deslacraram a prisão glacial que prendia Tekeli-li. Após décadas de cativeiro e fome, o vampiro está mais uma vez livre para deixar as cavernas e assombrar Icewind Dale. Primeiro, no entanto, ele planeja se alimentar. O vampiro é o único monstro errante que os personagens encontram nas Cavernas da Fome, e é provável que eles o encontrem mais de uma vez enquanto descem mais fundo nas cavernas.

O orbe do professor de @creature[Vellynne Harpell|IDRotF], @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF], é um especialista em vampiros e vampirismo. Assim que toma conhecimento de Tekeli-li, o orbe do professor compartilha as fraquezas conhecidas do vampiro com os personagens.

O grupo deve encontrar Tekeli-li várias vezes. A estratégia do vampiro é drenar os recursos do grupo, depois recuar e se recuperar antes de atacar novamente. Se Tekeli-li sofrer mais de 20 de dano durante um encontro, o vampiro se transforma em névoa e se retira para um local seguro na masmorra. Mesmo quando o vampiro não está imediatamente presente, sua risada cacarejante ecoa pelas cavernas esporadicamente.

Você pode organizar um encontro com Tekeli-li em qualquer lugar que desejar. Se você não tiver certeza de onde ele pode estar ou como ele deve se comportar, role um [[/r d6]] e consulte a tabela Localização de Tekeli-li. A tabela determina onde o vampiro é encontrado pela primeira vez. Se o vampiro recuar, role novamente na tabela para determinar para onde ele vai (role novamente se o resultado indicar que não houve mudança em sua localização).

Se os personagens não tiverem encontrado o vampiro quando chegarem a @UUID[.0000000000000016#h36.-frozen-grove]{área H36}, ele os ataca lá.

Caves of Hunger Locations (H1-H10)

The following locations are keyed to map 6.1.

H1. Into the Glacier

The light blue area shown on map 6.1 indicates the section of glacial wall that parts when the characters use the "Rime of the Frostmaiden" to cut a path to the dungeon. Reading the poem creates an opening in the ice wall that's 100 feet high, 30 feet wide, and 20 feet deep. A tunnel at the back of this opening contains uneven, naturally formed steps that descend to area H2.

If any of the Elk tribesfolk are present at this point, they volunteer to guard this entrance until the characters return or until hunger forces them to abandon their post.

H2. Crashed Skycoach

You come to a thirty-foot-high cavern. In its center, the remains of a wooden ship hull rest on the floor.

If @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] is allowed to view the wreckage, the professor orb offers the following commentary:

"Ah, a Netherese skycoach! I've always wanted to ride on one of those. Pity it's no longer in one piece."

The ship is all that remains of a Netherese skycoach, an airship that was used to ferry people to and from the floating city of Ythryn. Any loud disturbances in this cave cause the four @creature[Flameskull||flameskulls] in area H3 to cackle madly as they make their way to this cavern. The flameskulls are the animated, fire-wreathed skulls of Netherese wizards transformed into guardians by the will of the Frostmaiden.

The tunnel that leads to @UUID[.0000000000000016#h5.-icicle-drop]{area H5} has a slippery, 20-foot-tall drop-off that characters can descend safely with a successful [[/save ability=str dc=15]] (&Reference[skill=Athletics]) check. On a failed check, a character slips and lands &Reference[condition=prone], taking damage from the fall as normal.

Treasure. Characters who search the wreckage find the headless skeletons of four dead Netherese wizards and a 200-pound iron safe with a combination lock that has numbers from 0 to 50 written in the Draconic script. A character using thieves' tools can spend 1 minute trying to open the safe, doing so with a successful [[/save ability=dex dc=20]] check. The safe contains a @item[bag of holding], inside which are four amethysts (100 gp each) and a @item[ring of the ram] made of chardalyn (see the "@UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000000#chardalyn]{Chardalyn}" sidebar). The ring's unusual composition has no effect on its magical properties.

H3. Flameskulls

These two 15-foot-high caves and passageways are occupied by four @creature[Flameskull||flameskulls]—two in the caves west of area H2 and two in the caves northeast of area H2. They attack intruders on sight. The caves are otherwise empty.

H4. Kobold Vampire Spawn

Characters coming from area H2 must descend a naturally formed staircase of ice to reach this cave.

Frost-covered blocks of stone jut from the floor of this ten-foot-high cave of ice. Perched atop the largest stone is an emaciated kobold with glowing red eyes. It bares elongated fangs as it hisses at you, then scampers away.

The creature is a @creature[kobold vampire spawn|IDRotF] (see @UUID[.0000000000000021#kobolds]{appendix C}) created by Tekeli-li. @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] recognizes the creature as a vampire and knows its weaknesses. The undead kobold hungers for fresh blood but knows better than to attack a superior force. It tries to rejoin its kin in @UUID[.0000000000000016#h17.-den-of-the-vampire-spawn]{area H17}.

H5. Icicle Drop

Sparkling icicles as long as longswords cling to the forty-foot-high roof of this cavern, the floor of which is strewn with frost-covered blocks of stone and a toppled pillar. Embedded in the east wall is a glittering stone tower that appears to have crashed through the ceiling long ago. The exposed wall is cracked and has a few small holes in it but is otherwise intact.

If @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] is allowed to view the tower, the professor orb offers the following commentary:

"That tower appears to be of Netherese construction. Were it not for the cracks and holes, the stone would be smooth as glass, shaped by wizardly magic!"

Seven @creature[Ice Mephit||ice mephits] created by Auril cling to the ceiling, their wings wrapped tightly around their bodies so that they are easily mistaken for icicles. Characters who have passive Wisdom (&Reference[skill=Perception]) scores of 13 or higher can distinguish the mephits from the scores of real icicles that festoon the ceiling.

The ice mephits are tasked with guarding the cave but do not enter melee combat willingly. An ice mephit's preferred tactic is to use its action to fly next to an icicle and break it off, causing it to fall on an enemy below. Any creature standing under a falling icicle must succeed on a [[/save ability=dex dc=12]] saving throw or take 7 ([[/r 2d6]]) piercing damage from it.

Tekeli-li. If Tekeli-li is hiding in area H6, the @creature[gnoll vampire|IDRotF] (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{appendix C}) attacks the party after the ice mephits are dealt with, passing through the tower wall in mist form and assuming its Large hyena form. See the "@UUID[.0000000000000016#tekeli-li$4]{Tekeli-li}" section earlier in this chapter for more information.

H6. Ruined Tower

Characters who peer through the cracks and holes in the tower walls see the following:

Little remains of this tower besides its outer shell. The interior walls are decorated with shifting geometric patterns. A human skeleton in a white robe lies on the uneven floor.

If @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] is allowed to view the interior, the professor orb offers the following commentary:

"Netherese spellcasters would come to towers like this one to regain their spent magic more quickly. Despite the extensive damage, I would venture to guess that the tower still works!"

A character who examines the shifting geometric patterns can, with a successful [[/save ability=int dc=17]] (&Reference[skill=Arcana]) check, come to the same conclusion as Professor Skant.

The characters can punch a hole in the tower's outer wall that's big enough for them to crawl or walk through. Any character who takes a short rest in the tower regains one spell slot of 3rd level or lower at the end of that rest. A character can't gain this benefit again for 24 hours.

Treasure. The skeleton is the remains of a Netherese priest. It wears a silver holy symbol shaped like a four-pointed star (25 gp). A successful [[/save ability=int dc=15]] (&Reference[skill=History]) check identifies it as a holy symbol of Mystryl (see the "Mystryl" sidebar).

H7. Rubble-Strewn Cave

Aside from the tower embedded in the west wall (see area 6) and the rubble around it, this 30-foot-high cavern is empty. A naturally formed staircase to the southeast descends to @UUID[.0000000000000016#h10.-toppled-spire]{area H10}.

Tekeli-li. If Tekeli-li is hiding in area H6, the @creature[gnoll vampire|IDRotF] (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{appendix C}) passes through the tower wall in mist form, assumes its Large hyena form, and attacks the characters as they cross the cave or get within 10 feet of the tower. See the "@UUID[.0000000000000016#tekeli-li$4]{Tekeli-li}" section earlier in this chapter for more information.

Mystryl

p220

The history and fate of the god Mystryl are tied up in the story of Netheril's fall. Mystryl was the original god of magic. She is said to have created the Weave, which enables spellcasting. A Netherese mage named Karsus sought to steal Mystryl's godly power. To prevent certain doom from befalling the material world, Mystryl sacrificed herself to keep Karsus from achieving his goal. After her sacrifice, Mystryl was reincarnated as Mystra and is now worshiped across Faerûn. For more information about Netheril and its fall, see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000017]{chapter 7}.

H8. Arcane Eye Activator

A flat, slightly canted slab of stone rests in the center of this twenty-foot-high cave. One side is rough, as if the slab had been torn from a larger structure. The slab is eight feet long, four feet wide, four feet high, and covered with frost.

Scraping off the frost on the slab reveals Draconic script etched into its top, with an open eye carved above it. Close examination reveals that the pupil of the eye is a stone button that can be pressed. A @spell[detect magic] spell reveals an aura of divination magic around the slab.

If @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] sees the script, the professor orb informs the characters that the Netherese used the Draconic script (see the "@UUID[.0000000000000016#loross:-the-netherese-tongue]{Loross: The Netherese Tongue}" sidebar). If the characters don't know Draconic, Professor Skant can translate the script for them. It reads:

"Take the free, self-guided tour of Ythryn. Press the eye to cast the spell."

A character can use an action to press the eye, thereby using the slab to cast the @spell[arcane eye] spell. The spell lasts for 1 hour or until the character's concentration on the spell ends. The slab, which used to function whenever the button was pressed, is damaged. Once the spell is cast, the slab ceases to be magical.

H9. Golem Head

Lying on its side in the middle of this twenty-foot-high cave is an enormous head made of iron, its eyes aglow with a golden light.

Iron golems were used as sentries throughout Ythryn. This head is all that remains of a golem that was decapitated when the city crashed. It has been separated from the city and its body ever since. The potent magic used in the golem's construction has kept the head functional.

The head can't attack or use its Poison Breath in this state, nor can it speak. It can communicate by blinking the lights in its eyes—one blink for no, two blinks for yes, three blinks for "I don't know." It understands Elvish and Loross (see the "Loross: The Netherese Tongue" sidebar). If the characters know none of these languages, Professor Skant can communicate with the golem in Loross on their behalf.

Here are examples of the questions the party might ask, and the golem's responses:

  • "Do you know where Ythryn is?" (No.)
  • "Were you damaged when the city fell?" (Yes.)
  • "Is there danger ahead?" (I don't know.)
  • "Have other creatures passed through this cave recently?" (Yes. The golem is referring to the kobold vampire spawn that lair in the caves to the east.)

Loross: the Netherese Tongue

p221

Loross, a language spoken by the Netherese, is a dead tongue. Very few creatures in the world today would recognize it, much less speak it. Loross used the Draconic alphabet in its written form, while the spoken language had many similarities with Elvish, due to the cultural influence the elves had on Netherese civilization. A character who speaks Elvish can understand what a creature speaking Loross is saying, and vice versa. A character who has the Cloistered Scholar or Sage background, or a warlock who knows the Eyes of the Rune Keeper invocation, can translate written Loross without an ability check.

On a failed save, the creature stands perfectly still and does nothing while the alien entity probes its mind for 1 hour. If a creature remains under the spell's effect for the full duration, it must make a [[/save ability=int dc=15]] saving throw at the end of the hour, taking 44 ([[/r 8d10]]) psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Whether the save succeeds or fails, a creature subjected to the hour-long probe also gains a randomly determined form of long-term madness, determined by rolling on the @table[long-term madness||Long-Term Madness table] in the Dungeon Master's Guide.

The ice wall has AC 13, vulnerability to fire damage, and immunity to cold, poison, and psychic damage. If the wall takes 50 points of damage or more, large cracks form along its surface, and the alien entity withdraws into its distant demiplane, never to return. As it withdraws, its @spell[dominate monster] spells end.

H32. Mirrored Cavern

Every surface of this twenty-foot-high cave is polished to a mirror finish, such that you are faced with countless distorted reflections of yourself. Your head spins as you try to keep your bearings.

If Tekeli-li is here, add:

Crouching in the middle of the cavern is a gaunt gnoll with ravenous eyes and the foul stench of the grave coming from it. The creature does not cast a reflection.

Ranged attack rolls made in this cave have disadvantage because of the disorienting reflections. If Tekeli-li is here, the @creature[gnoll vampire|IDRotF] (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{appendix C}) uses its Frightful Cackle first, then targets enemies with its Sickening Gaze until one or more them closes to melee range. See the "@UUID[.0000000000000016#tekeli-li$4]{Tekeli-li}" section earlier in this chapter for more of Tekeli-li's tactics.

Slaad Host. If one of the characters has the Slaad Host secret (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000020]{appendix B}) and the @creature[slaad tadpole] hasn't been born or destroyed yet, it bursts from the character's chest when the character enters this cavern.

H33. Watcher in the Walls

This fifteen-foot-high, U-shaped cavern appears empty at first glance, but a sudden tapping draws your attention to the west wall, within which is trapped a black-robed figure with a golden visage.

A @creature[mummy] dressed in a tattered black robe and wearing a golden face mask is trapped in the western wall and has no way to free itself from the ice. The mummy was created by Netherese priests to serve as a lore-keeper in Ythryn.

The section of ice that traps the mummy has AC 13, 22 hit points, vulnerability to fire damage, and immunity to cold, poison, and psychic damage. If the wall is reduced to 0 hit points, the mummy is released and makes its way to @UUID[.0000000000000016#h39.-passage-to-ythryn]{area H39} and from there to the library in Ythryn (@UUID[.0000000000000017#y9.-library]{area Y9} in @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000017]{chapter 7}). It ignores the characters unless they interfere with it.

Treasure. The mummy's golden mask (750 gp) is a Netherese art object.

H34. City Debris

This cave might contain a psychic haunting (see "@UUID[.0000000000000016#psychic-hauntings]{Psychic Hauntings}").

Large pieces of stone jut from the twenty-foot-high ceiling of this empty cavern.

The stone fragments are pieces of Ythryn that settled in the ice. Any character who examines the stone and makes a successful [[/save ability=int dc=15]] (&Reference[skill=History]) check to discern its true nature. (A dwarf's Stonecunning trait can prove helpful here.) If the check succeeds, the character can confirm that the stone is neither naturally formed nor hewn, but rather shaped by magic.

H35. Mysterious Cube

On the floor in the middle of this fifteen-foot-high cave is an eight-foot cube of smooth, grayish-green stone that is free of frost.

The stone cube is a @creature[spitting mimic|IDRotF] (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{appendix C}). Anything that touches the mimic automatically adheres to it, and a Huge or smaller creature adhered to the mimic is also &Reference[condition=grappled] by it (escape DC 16). The mimic sprouts pseudopods and attacks if it is touched, harmed, or otherwise disturbed.

H36. Frozen Grove

If the characters make it this far and haven't yet faced Tekeli-li, the @creature[gnoll vampire|IDRotF] (see @UUID[JournalEntry.bGFHJOs6yQKK0dQn.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000021]{appendix C}) confronts them here in its Large hyena form.

A grove of frost-covered trees fills this fifty-foot-high cavern, the floor of which is a mixture of earth and ice. By some magic or miracle, the trees are alive, their canopies thick with frosty leaves, their branches bearing purple pears that hang like ornaments. A low wall, broken in several places, threads its way through the grove, which is surrounded by six exits.

Purple Pear

This grove of pear trees was enchanted by Netherese arcanists to endure high altitude and harsh weather. That magic has enabled the trees to thrive even in these icy caverns. Essential to the grove's survival is a @creature[dryad] named Hathowyn, who resides in the southernmost tree. If the dryad dies, the trees die [[/r 1d10]] days later. Centuries of isolation have made Hathowyn lonely, and the dryad hungers for conversation and companionship.

If Tekeli-li is present, Hathowyn remains hidden in her tree until the gnoll vampire leaves or is destroyed. Tekeli-li has never seen the dryad and is unaware of her presence. If given the chance, Hathowyn tries to engage the characters in conversation, speaking Elvish or Sylvan. She hopes to keep them in the grove for as long as possible. If the characters try to leave, Hathowyn tries to charm one of them into staying and keeping her company. The dryad knows the following information:

  • Netherese magic binds the dryad to the grove and keeps the trees alive.
  • The pears hanging from the trees are magically delicious.
  • Three dark elves have passed through the dryad's grove on multiple occasions, coming and going through the southwest and southeast passages (leading to areas @UUID[.0000000000000016#h29.-east-observation-platform]{H29} and @UUID[.0000000000000016#h39.-passage-to-ythryn]{H39}). Hathowyn hasn't spoken to them out of fear that they might harm her.
  • Strange, one-eyed creatures lurk in the cave to the south (@UUID[.0000000000000016#h37.-weird-insights]{area H37}). They followed the dark elves up from below after their last foray down the southeast passage.

Purple Pears. Each tree has [[/r 3d6]] ripe pears hanging from its branches, most of them within reach. Any creature that eats more than half of a purple pear gains the benefit of a special charm (see "Supernatural Gifts" in the Dungeon Master's Guide). Determine the charm randomly by rolling a [[/r d20]] and consulting the Purple Pear Charms table. This charm must vanish from a creature before it can gain any more charms by eating purple pears. A creature that eats three or more purple pears within 1 hour is &Reference[condition=poisoned] for 24 hours thereafter and suffers painful cramps until the condition ends.

H37. Weird Insights

Huddled together at the west end of this ten-foot-high cave are four hunched creatures with spiny backs and sharp claws. Each one stares at you with a single unblinking eye.

These four @creature[Nothic||nothics], evil denizens of Ythryn, followed Yilsebek and his fellow drow as far as the dryad's grove (area H36) before taking refuge in this cave. The nothics use their Weird Insight to glean secrets from the characters. They are reluctant to provoke a well-armed group of adventurers, preferring to pick off lone prey, but if attacked, they respond in kind.

Offering food and drink to the nothics loosens their tongues, but these ones speak and understand only Loross (see the "@UUID[.0000000000000016#loross:-the-netherese-tongue]{Loross: The Netherese Tongue}"). They can also write in the Draconic script. @item[Professor Skant|IDRotF] can serve as a translator if none of the characters can understand the nothics.

The nothics have no memory of their lost humanity, and any information they might provide is garbled with bits of nonsense. Characters who succeed on a [[/save ability=wis dc=12]] (&Reference[skill=Insight]) check can glean the following information from the nothics after a brief conversation:

  • Iriolarthas, a powerful Netherese archwizard, watches over Ythryn. He has lost not only his mind, but most of his body as well. All that remains of him is a floating human skull.
  • Giant floating hands made of magical force wander around in the city.
  • Ythryn's mythallar won't function properly as long as the Spindle—a powerful artifact dating back 40,000 years—remains in the city. The artifact is located within the city's central spire.
  • One must perform a ritual to enter Ythryn's central spire. (The nothics don't remember how the ritual works, but they do recall that clues can be found in the towers of wizardry scattered throughout the city.)
  • Somewhere in Ythryn is a scroll that can summon the tarrasque.

Wrapping Up

Once the characters enter area H39, they can make their way deeper inside the Reghed Glacier, where the lost city of Ythryn awaits.

Avarice Follows

Guided by the visions of Levistus, @creature[Avarice|IDRotF] follows the characters to the Reghed Glacier. Around the time when the characters reach Ythryn, the tiefling wizard and her followers arrive at the Caves of Hunger and kill any Elk Tribe warriors guarding the entrance. @creature[Avarice|IDRotF] then leads her troupe through the icy caverns, marching toward her destiny.

More information about @creature[Avarice|IDRotF] and her followers is provided in the next chapter.

Tekeli-li Escapes

If the characters do not slay Tekeli-li, the gnoll vampire leaves the Caves of Hunger in mist form and makes its way across the sunless tundra, eventually finding an ample food supply in Ten-Towns.
